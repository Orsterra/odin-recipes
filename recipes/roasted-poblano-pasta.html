Roasted Poblano Pasta Recipes

Description

This roasted poblano pasta has a creamy sauce with roasted chiles, cilantro, and garlic. It is a very fun take on roasted pepper sauce, served over shell pasta. The sauce, blended in a food processor, is also a great spread for toasted bread.

Ingridients

  • 2 medium poblano peppers
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 cup packed cilantro leaves
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 8 ounces medium shell pasta
  • 1/8 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven’s broiler, and place a rack about 6 inches below the heating element. Line a baking sheet with foil; place peppers on the sheet and place under the broiler. Broil until peppers are fully charred, turning occasionally. Remove from the oven; place peppers in a plastic bag, sealing loosely. Let steam in the bag for 5 to 10 minutes.
  2. Remove peppers from the bag and place on a work surface. Peel off charred skin, then slit in half. Gently remove all the seeds and spine. Place peppers in a food processor. Add salt, cumin, olive oil, cilantro, chicken stock, cream, and garlic. Pulse to process until smooth.
  3. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook until al dente, about 8 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  4. Taste the sauce, add pepper flakes, then process again. If too spicy, add a bit more cream or sour cream to temper. Toss with pasta and garnish with additional cilantro.
